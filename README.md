**Vectoreek** is a specialized AI robotics system that rejects the need for independent locomotion. Instead of building robots with wheels or legs, Vectoreek creates **Symbiont Modules** designed to infiltrate and "hijack" the power and movement of existing infrastructure.

## 🚀 The Vision
Most robots fail because locomotion is energy-expensive and mechanically complex. Vectoreek solves this by being a **parasite**. 

A Vectoreek module is a compact AI core with high-strength universal adhesion pads (magnetic, vacuum, and micro-hook). It waits at a "junction" until a suitable **Host** (a conveyor belt, a delivery robot, an HVAC fan, or even a vehicle) passes by. It then latches on, harvests energy from the host's vibration or rotation, and travels for free.

## 🛠 Key Innovations
1. **Proxy Locomotion Pathfinding:** The AI doesn't calculate "how do I walk there?" but "which moving object is going my way?"
2. **Kinetic Parasitism:** Energy harvesting modules that turn a host's mechanical waste (vibration/heat) into battery power.
3. **Low-Signature Infiltration:** By moving *with* the environment, Vectoreek modules avoid traditional motion detection and thermal signatures.

## 📁 Project Structure
- `vectoreek/engine.py`: Host simulation and latching physics.
- `vectoreek/ai.py`: Symbiote decision logic and host utility scoring.
- `docs/SYMBIO_PROTOCOLS.md`: Technical guide on host identification.
- `main.py`: Simulation of a module hijacking factory infrastructure.

## 🚦 Quick Start
Watch the Vectoreek module hop between hosts to reach its goal:
